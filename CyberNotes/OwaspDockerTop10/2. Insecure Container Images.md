# Insecure Container Images
Are you aware that using insecure container images can lead to security vulnerabilities in your applications?

Insecure container images can be used as vector for attack, meaning that if an attacker can compromise the container image, they can gain access to your applications.

By ensuring that the container images you use are secure, you can prevent attackers from compromising your applications and protect sensitive data.

Implement security measures for container images, such as image scanning and signature validation.

Additionally, consider implementing a process for regularly updating and patching container images to ensure that any known vulnerabilities are addressed.

Additionally, you can use container registries that have built-in security features, such as scanning and signing images. These registries can also provide transparency and traceability, allowing you to easily track the origin and distribution of your container images.

Another best practices is to use minimal base images that contain only the necessary libraries and dependencies, as it helps you to reduce the attack surface and increase security.

It's also important to consider the supply chain of the images you use, ensuring that you only use images from trusted sources and that you are aware of the origin and distribution of those images. This will help you to protect your environment from the use of compromised or malicious images.

In summary by implementing security measures for container images and being aware of the origin and distribution of the images, you can prevent attackers from compromising your applications and protect sensitive data.
